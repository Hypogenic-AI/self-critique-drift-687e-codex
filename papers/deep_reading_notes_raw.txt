A R ELATED WORKS During LLM inference, self-correction (SC) refers to the ability to iteratively improve future responses by reflecting on the errors from previous responses. We refer readers to an excellent survey on the correction mechanism of LLMs (Pan et al., 2023). Self-correction characteristics in Large Language Models can be generally achieved by two approaches: intrinsic SC and extrinsic SC. In this section, we provide a brief survey on the current techniques used on both sides, with a focus on the intrinsic self-correction ability, which is also the focus of the main discussion of this paper. A.1 E XTRINSIC SC In the current LLM community, extrinsic self-correction (SC) refers to large language models (LLMs) using external sources of knowledge to refine their own answers. This process enables the models to improve the accuracy and reliability of their responses by cross-referencing and correcting potential errors based on updated or specialized information. Such an idea could be traced back to decades ago when external information was used to guide a deep learning (DL) model to generate more accurate answers (Gou et al., 2021; Hinton et al., 2015).
